ELEPHANT SEAL MOVEMENTS: MODELLING MIGRATION

Introduction

Random walk is a path of succession of random steps, which mathematical formalized. We will examine the movements of elephant seals migrations applying random walk model. However, the movement is on the surface of a sphere, which has a possibility that elephant seal migrates along great-circle path.

Before we examine random walk on great circle, let us look at several different models of random walk which are 'One Dimensional Simple Random Walk', 'One Dimensional Brownian Random Walk', 'Two Dimensional Simple Random Walk', 'Two Dimensional Brownian Random Walk', 'Two Dimensional Random Walk with Drift', then finally 'Great Circle Random Walk'.

When the number of steps in the discrete random walk is large, the discretization becomes less obvious, and takes on a fractal-like pattern. That is, for a large number of steps, the overall picture no longer depends on the scale of the random walk. The migration of elephant seals shows significant elements of randomness, although the destination appears to be fixed. Thus we can model this migration using a similar walk as the one above, but on a sphere. The resulting model is a random walk on a sphere that moves randomly in both horizontal and vertical directions, but also drifts toward its destination. This drift acts along the shortest path from the current point to the destination, which on a sphere is a great circle instead of a line.
